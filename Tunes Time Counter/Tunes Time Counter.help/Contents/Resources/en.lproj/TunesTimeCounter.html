iTunes and Apple Music

A question you could ask: I have subscribed to Apple Music. Will this app tell me the time I spent listening tracks that are not in my library?
Short answer: No. And there is nothing I can do about it, sorry.

Computation Time

Another question you could ask would be: So I have understood that you do a simple multiplication and addition for each of my tracks in my library. This seems pretty simple, it should be fast! How come the app takes forever to load the info I want?
Yes, the computations are fast, indeed. However, Apple Events communication is not. It is actually really slow. Even more so since a few releases of macOS (somewhere around Sierra). Apple added a lot of security and privacy features to macOS, which is great! However, it also slowed the Apple Events inter-process communication.

Zero-Length Tracks

If you get a lot of tracks whose length is 0, you can hide them in the preferences. The change take effect at the next refresh.
Usually these tracks are radio stations and other live tracks that are not correctly detected as non-real tracks and are thus displayed in the list of tracks.

UI Customization

In the window with the list of tracks, you can choose to show or hide the Artist, Album and Composer columns. Simply right-click the table-view header and choose the column you want to show or hide.

Refreshing the List of Tracks

If you want to get more up-to-date list of tracks and listen time after having listened to iTunes for a few hours, you can click the little arrow at the bottom left of the window.
